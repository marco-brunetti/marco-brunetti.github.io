Mini Tanks

In 2020, at the height of the pandemic, I decided to improve my skills by creating my own personal game jams. I dedicated 48-hour sprints to making and finishing games, one after another. My goal was to enhance my game design skills and avoid getting stuck in long, poorly coded mechanics. I aimed to get things done within a tight time budget. I finished six mini-games in a row for this project, with the last and best one being Mini Tanks. For this game, I decided to keep the mechanics simple and achieve a distinct visual style. Before this game, I struggled to find a concise visual style for my games.

Achievements:

  • Visuals: I created my first game with a decent visual style.
  • Powerups: It was my first game to feature in-game modifiers.
  • Time management: I adapted my game development cycle to professional time budgets.

Failures:

  • Coding skills: I failed to achieve a performant build for web play, resulting in noticeable in-game stutters.
  • 3D asset optimization: I had little experience with third-party models, and some of them had a high vertex count, contributing to the game stutter problem.
  • UI: It was one of my first attempts to create a good UI, but more work was needed to achieve that.
